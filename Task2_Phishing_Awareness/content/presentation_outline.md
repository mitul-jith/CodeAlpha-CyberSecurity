## Slide 2

What is Phishing?

Definition

Phishing is a cyberattack in which attackers impersonate a trusted individual, organization, or service to trick victims into revealing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, banking details, or other confidential data.

Key Characteristics
Pretends to be a trusted source.
Creates urgency or fear.
Requests sensitive information.
Uses deceptive links or attachments.
Attempts to manipulate human behavior.
Example

An email claiming to be from a bank asks a user to verify their account through a provided link. The link redirects to a fake website designed to steal login credentials.

Learning Objective

Understand the basic concept of phishing and recognize why it remains one of the most successful cyberattacks.

## Slide 3

### Why Phishing is Dangerous

### Impact on Individuals

* Theft of usernames and passwords.
* Financial loss through banking fraud.
* Identity theft and account compromise.
* Loss of personal and sensitive information.

### Impact on Organizations

* Data breaches.
* Financial damage.
* Reputational loss.
* Operational disruption.
* Legal and regulatory consequences.

### Why Attackers Prefer Phishing

* Low cost to execute.
* Targets human behavior instead of technology.
* Can reach thousands of victims simultaneously.
* Often bypasses technical security controls.

### Key Fact

A single successful phishing email can provide attackers with access to an entire organization's network.


## Slide 4

How Attackers Trick Victims
Social Engineering Techniques
Urgency ("Act immediately")
Fear ("Account suspended")
Authority ("Message from IT Department")
Curiosity ("Confidential salary report")
Rewards ("You won a prize")
Attack Process
Create a convincing message.
Gain the victim's trust.
Trigger an emotional response.
Obtain credentials or sensitive data.
Key Message

Attackers exploit human psychology more often than technical vulnerabilities.

## Slide 5
Common Types of Phishing

Email Phishing

Mass fraudulent emails sent to many users.

Spear Phishing

Highly targeted attacks against specific individuals.

Whaling

Attacks targeting executives and senior management.

Smishing

Phishing through SMS messages.

Vishing

Phishing through phone calls.

QR Phishing (Quishing)

Malicious QR codes leading to fake websites.

## Slide 6
Anatomy of a Phishing Email

Warning Signs
Suspicious sender address
Generic greeting
Urgent language
Unexpected attachments
Suspicious links
Grammar and spelling errors
Example Indicators

From: support-paypal-security@example.com

Subject: Urgent Account Verification Required

Key Message

Always verify the sender, links, and request before taking action.

## Slide 7
Fake Websites and Login Pages

Characteristics
Similar appearance to legitimate websites
Fake login forms
Look-alike domain names
HTTPS may still be present
Example

Legitimate:
www.paypal.com

Malicious:
www.paypaI-security.com

Key Message

Always verify the website URL before entering credentials.

## Slide 9
How to Identify Phishing Attempts

Checklist

✓ Verify sender address

✓ Inspect URLs carefully

✓ Avoid unexpected attachments

✓ Be cautious of urgent requests

✓ Verify requests through official channels

✓ Check for spelling and grammar mistakes

Key Message

Pause, Verify, Then Act.

Security awareness training
Email filtering solutions
Incident reporting procedures
Regular security assessments
